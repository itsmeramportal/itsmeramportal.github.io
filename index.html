Snapshot

Naripeddi Ramchandra Rao, generally referred to as just Ram, is a hands-on-media professional and an independent consultant from a journalistic background, with over 25 years of distinguished experience in Corporate Communications and Public Relations, with a well-established pan Indian network within Government, Political, Corporate and Media circles. Ram's services have been successfully used by scores of politicians, businessmen, socialites and celebrities for several years at the highest level, and his liaising and PR abilities are acknowledged as one of the best in the Telugu States. He is also the Managing Director of Rammedia Pvt. Ltd. and is on the Board of Directors of GSOWN Lifestyle Centres, a well-reputed company in timeshare and vacation industry and a division of Majors Group.

Several corporates valued Thousands of Crores have seen their fortunes owing to Ram’s sheer strategic management and liaising skills. His close association with several politicians in power such as Ministers, MPs and MLAs both at state and central level and high level bureaucrats at Secretary, Commissioner and Director levels, is one of his chief strengths which could be leveraged for facilitating contracts or other purposes. He is one of the most sought for Consultants on a repertoire of subjects, such as political issues, governmental matters, corporate administration, tender processing, legal matters, financial matters, media associations and so on.

Background

Ram hails from Kotikalapudi, a remote hamlet near Addanki in Prakasam District of Andhra Pradesh. With an agricultural parentage and a very basic economic background, though belonging to a much respected community, Ram worked strenuously for over three decades to reach his present stature of respect and renown.

Having completed his intermediate in Addanki town, he later finished his Graduation in Arts at Andhra University and found government employment as an LDC in the Endowments Department. After a year of service, he found it to be least interesting and bereft of anything challenging. So, he quit and was offered a job as a Teleprinter in Andhra Jyothi, one of the leading Telugu Dailies of the time. Here he learnt the magic of drafting press reports under the supervision of his superior, Late Shri AVS, who later became a leading comedy actor in Telugu Film Industry.

Ram’s drafting skills in Telugu were much appreciated, and he soon found himself promoted as a Staff Reporter. Within a few years he worked himself up with his brilliant skills and communication, and also later worked for the Indian Express, and the Times of India. During his tenure with all these newspapers, he developed excellent public relations with a number of politicians, bureaucrats, businessmen, film personalities, socialites and other celebrities. His Telugu drafts drew even the attention of the then CM of combined AP Shri N. Chandrababu Naidu and he was summoned for a one to one interaction with him on certain political matters.

His press experience and his own unique way of interacting with people led him to become one of the most sought for consultants for various VIPs in the State. After having put in nearly fifteen years of experience in print media, he joined Lanco Group Chairman Shri Lagadapati Rajgopal, and worked with him as his personal advisor on various matters including political and corporate for over two years. He presently works with several VIPs and Corporates and also takes up independent assignments as a Business Consultant and Political Strategist.

Down the Memory Lane

After his primary schooling in the village itself, between his 5th and 10th Standards, he would walk each day from his village to the high school, which was about 4.5 kms, without even footwear, since they were not affordable by his parents.

In the summer when the sands would be hot, students like him would use large leaves of trees to protect themselves from the hot earth, by running for some time, and when their feet would become hot, throw the leaves down and jump on them till they cooled down. This was one of the most trying childhood experiences he had. And then he had to cross the Gundlakamma River which was about one kilometre wide in chest deep water every day to reach school. These experiences in his formative years demonstrate his humility and understanding of the economic status of all due to which he helped governments reach out to the lowest income classes for improving their lives.
